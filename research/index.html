Research

Firm Growth under Uncertainty

This project aims to study how sensitive startups' financing and scaling is to aggregate uncertainty. Leveraging firm-level balance sheet data from 2015 to 2024, I aim to measure the impact of economic uncertainty on funding flows to high-growth enterprises. I then plan to develop a general equilibrium model in which financial intermediaries can offer equity or debt contracts to young firms, and use it to identify the conditions under which increased uncertainty could lead to a reduced offering of equity contracts.

Status: Data construction and preliminary analysis

Data: Moody's ORBIS Europe

Sign Restrictions under Information Channel Distortions

Work by Canova and De Nicoló (2002) and Uhlig (2005) pioneered the sign restriction identification approach in Structural Vector Autoregression models. The assumption is that macroeconomic fundamentals can be exploited to infer which part of a comovement of macro time series and policy shocks is down to and information channel distortion. Increased awareness of this information channel can be exploited by the announcing authority, leading to a potential distortion of the identification.

Motivation: Timing of announcements

Status: Dataset build and theoretical properties exploration

Data sources: News articles (FACTIVA), Social Media announcements
